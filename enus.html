about

history

The history of South Korea, after researched through anthropology, found that humans lived on the Korean peninsula as early as 700,000 years ago. This period is called the prehistoric era. According to the legend, the mythical figure Tan Jun established the first kingdom of Korea in the 2333 BC ~ ancient North Korea. Later, several tribes moved from the southern part of Manchuria to the Korean peninsula. This period is called ancient North Korea. The Joseon Dynasty was established in the late 14th century, and Confucianism became the state religion, which had a great influence on the whole society. In 1443, the Korean alphabet was invented. As a result of Japanese aggression (1592, Renchen chaos) and the aggression of foreign forces, North Korea began to decline. The historic South-North Korean summit in 2000 was held in Pyongyang, the capital of North Korea. After the partition of the DPRK and the ROK, South Korea experienced a repeated alternation of democracy and dictatorship.

culture

The North-South confrontation on the Korean peninsula after the Korean War led to differences in the modern socialization of the North and South Korea. Yet, the traditional culture of the two sides was in the same line. The modern social culture of Korea was derived from the combination of Korean traditional culture and modern society's popular culture. Since the North and South Korea confrontation in 1948, the modern culture of South and North Korea has developed differently. Korean modern social culture is the product of the modernization of Korean national culture. With the development of the Korean economy and society, Korean lifestyles such as food, clothing, housing and transportation have also changed, thus building a modern Korean culture. The popularity of Korean culture in Asia and the world is known as the Korean Wave.

economic

The Republic of Korea’s economy is one of the world’s major economies, a member of the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) and one of the four Asian tigers. South Korea is also the only developed country in the next 11 countries. According to the statistics of the World Bank, South Korea's GDP in 2014 ranked 13th in the world in terms of international exchange rates and relative purchasing power indicators. South Korea is the world's sixth largest exporter and the ninth largest importer. As of March 2015, South Korea has signed 14 free trade agreements with 52 countries including the United States, the European Union, and China. Its “economic territory” has reached 73.5% of world GDP. Since the 1960s, South Korea has The country's comparative advantage, and actively participate in the international division of labor, so that economic development has entered a rapid development track.

Korea Weather

spring

spring season: early March to late May

The temperature difference between day and night is big. Cool in the morning and evening, while it is warm during the day. Remember to bring thin long sleeves and a lightweight jacket. Spring is also the cherry blossom season in Korea.

fall

fall season: mid September to mid November

The wind starts to blow and there are more sunny weather, which you can go outside. The autumn is the same as spring, where the temperature difference between day and night is big. Remember to bring a jacket, scarf and scarves.

summer

summer season: early June to the end of September

The summer times are hot with high humidity. Please bring cool summer clothes and a light jacket to avoid the sudden changes in the weather. July to August is Korea's rainy season. Do not forget to bring some rain gears.

winter

winter season: Late November to the end of February

The temperatures in the winter times are below zero degrees Celsius, and the probability of snow is relatively high. Wear warm, thick clothing, scarves & gloves.

Korean Introduction

the history of Korean

Korean is used in the Korean peninsula and the languages ​​used in the Northeast of China, the Far East of Russia, Japan, and other Korean ethnic groups or Korean ethnic communities. In China In 1897, before the establishment of the Korean Empire, the language was only called "Korean" or "Dongyu." After the founding of the People's Republic of China in 1949, the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, which belongs to the socialist camp, was the sole legal government of the entire Korean peninsula. Therefore, all the words about the Korean culture and country in China and the North Korean Peninsula are referred to as "North Korea." The language thus continues to be called "Korean."
